Where did the Melanesians originate from?

These dark-skinned people with blonde hair and blue eyes originated from Africa. They migrated to the South Pacific more than 50,000 years ago. They relocated long before the European explorers arrived in the Pacific during the 16th century.

Black people with blonde hair are believed to be the descendants of the first Homosapiens. A study on the individuals revealed that the Melanesian population was linked to the first group of Homosapiens, which emigrated from Africa more than two thousand generations ago.

Researchers analysed the Y chromosome and mitochondrial DNA of Aboriginal Australians and Melanesians from New Guinea. Later, they compared the results with multiple DNA patterns related to early humans.

What causes blonde hair in Melanesians?

Melanesian blonde hair and blue eyes result from isolation from other populations, leading to interbreeding within their community. While black people naturally have dark hair, Melanesians have a range of hair colours, including:

  • Brown
  • Red
  • Blonde

Black people with blonde hair have fascinated many while prompting others to research more about them. From their extensive research, scientists found out that the gene responsible for blonde hair in Melanesians is different from the gene responsible for blonde hair in Europeans.

For the Melanesians, the blonde gene on their hair results from a genetic mutation several thousands of years ago. The mutation has no connection to the same gene, causing blondeness among Europeans.

What is the culture and traditions of Melanesians?

Black tribe with blue eyes and blonde hair has a rich culture with diverse and unique traditions passed down from generation to generation. There are extreme differences between rural and urban Melanesians, which include:

Interpersonal relations

Males from the highlands of Papua New Guinea greeted each other by rubbing each other's groin region. However, the Western handshake has replaced this traditional greeting among many people.

Also, most groups required the occurrence of marriage after a special courtship between men and women. After wooing the females, marriages are negotiated between families, and they involve a bride price paid by the son-in-law to the bride's father.

Religion

Due to the active missionaries in Melanesia, Christianity has widely spread throughout. The missionaries are actively learning the Melanesian’s native languages and translating the Bible into those languages.

Some native religions are still being practised, although in more modified forms. In the past, their original belief systems incorporated various aspects, such as cannibalism and headhunting. However, since the late 1920s, the two practices have been considered illegal.

Language

Melanesia is a linguistic frontier since there is more than one official national language in most islands where Melanesians live. For example, people from Papua New Guinea have three official languages: English, Tok Pisin, and Hiri Motu.

New Guinea Island has more than 1,000 different languages. Despite having such a large number of languages, some have as few as 50 speakers, while others have as many as a few hundred thousand. However, most of the languages have never been documented.

Family life

Melanesians experience a great deal of antagonism between males and females, resulting in having men's and women's houses in most villages. For instance, among the Sepik River people, men have ceremonial houses that are off-limit to all women and other uninitiated men.

On the other hand, women also play an important role in the Melanesian family lives. For instance, women are considered the primary food producers and caretakers of children. They also play a significant role in political and ceremonial aspects of the society.

Living conditions and social problems

Since Melanesians live in a tropical region, they experience various environmental challenges such as heat, rain, and mosquitoes. Healing takes a long time, so infection becomes a serious problem. However, the region is relatively healthy.

Melanesians have been dealing with various modern-world challenges, such as alcoholism. With easy access to money, some Melanesian males have indulged in alcohol. Also, due to the lack of condoms until recently, HIV/AIDS has been a serious threat among the Papua New Guinea people.

Gender issues

The relationships between men and women vary significantly within the Melanesian culture. Even though male initiation is dominant, female initiation is also found in several societies. For instance, male and female initiation is practised among the Abelam of Papua New Guinea.
