9 Must-Have Smart Gadgets for Your Garage

 Posted on February 9, 2024  |  5 minutes  |  1041 words  |  Aldo Pusey

Smartening your garage is one of the simplest ways of improving security, convenience, and safety. You can achieve this using smart gadgets like cameras for monitoring, a dehumidifier to remove moisture, and a parking sensor for better parking.

Here are some must-have smart gadgets for your garage.

1. Smart Garage Hub

Smart garage hubs allow you to operate your garage door remotely. For instance, Chamberlain's myQ Smart Garage Hub adds smart functionality to many garage door openers manufactured after 1993.

The garage hub enables remote monitoring of your garage door's status and controls its opening or closing using the myQ app on your smartphone. The app also lets you add users, create schedules, and receive real-time alerts.

2. Smart Garage Door Opener

Forgetting to close your garage door is akin to inviting home intruders. One way to prevent this risk is to install a garage door opener.

Smart garage door openers are designed to open and close garage doors using an app or voice assistants like Alexa, Siri, and Google Assistant.

There are several types of smart garage door openers, and many manufacturers design models that are easy to use.

For example, Chamberlain's myQ garage door openers let you operate your door using a smartphone app. They record door opening and closing times and allow sharing access with co-owners and guests. In addition, they work with Amazon Key In-Garage Delivery, allowing Amazon drivers to deliver packages inside your garage.

3. Garage Parking Sensor

Parking cars might be tricky if you don't have strong driving skills or share a garage with another vehicle. To prevent accidentally bumping into garage walls and the things inside, consider using a garage parking sensor.

A garage parking sensor removes the guesswork by providing precise instructions on when and where to stop. For instance, the Park Assist by Garage Smart is a sensor-activated, app-controlled parking assist device that enables you to park your car safely, even in tight garages.

4. Smart Plug

Smart plugs use Wi-Fi or similar technology to enable homeowners to turn practically any electronic device into a smart one. Simply plug it into an electrical outlet, add a connected device, and then configure it using an app. You can then remotely operate the connected device via a smartphone app or voice command.

Consider the TP-Link Kasa Smart Wi-Fi Outdoor Plug, for instance. It's both an indoor and outdoor smart plug that integrates with numerous smart devices, including other plugs, switches, and light bulbs.

The smart plug supports voice and remote control. You can pair it with voice assistants, like Alexa and Google Assistant, to control connected appliances or use the Kasa app on your smartphone to remotely manage devices. You can also set it to turn on/off automatically using schedules or a countdown timer.

5. Smart LED Lights

Sometimes, arriving home late means dealing with the hassle of driving through a dark driveway to reach a poorly lit garage, where you'll have to turn on the lights manually.

Motion-sensor LED lights illuminate driveways on demand and discourage garage intrusions. To illustrate, the Philips Hue Welcome Outdoor Floodlight emits bright light and can be controlled from a smartphone via the Philips Hue app. It's also weatherproof, making it suitable for all seasons.

6. Door Alarm

Setting up an alarm serves two purposes: discouraging potential thieves and receiving alerts for potential problems. Smart alarms, in particular, can send alerts to your smartphone. They could also be designed to work with existing garage doors.

For example, the Ring Alarm Security Kit is an easy-to-install and use smart home security system. It includes sensors to detect when doors open and is compatible with myQ, enabling remote control of Chamberlain and LiftMaster garage doors.

Those subscribed to a Ring Protect Plan can operate the alarm system using their voice (it's Alexa-compatible) and receive phone alerts when the system is triggered.

7. Smart Charging Station

Installing a smart charging station can make charging hassle-free, reliable, and economical. Take the Level 2 JuiceBox 40 smart EV charging station, for instance. It features a 25-foot cable and can charge EVs from various car manufacturers, including Teslas.

The charging station allows monitoring and scheduling of charging via a mobile app. Through the app, you can get plug-in reminders and full-charge alerts and set charging routines. JuiceBox 40 also works with Alexa, enabling you to control it through voice commands.

8. Smart Cameras

Strengthen your smart home's security by adding a surveillance camera to your garage. The myQ Smart Garage HD Camera is a prime example of a smart camera designed for garage use.

The Wi-Fi-enabled camera offers high dynamic range and night vision, ensuring superior image quality in various lighting conditions, and is controllable via the myQ smartphone app.

The smart camera supports live streaming, allowing you to view in-garage deliveries happening in real-time. In addition, it's compatible with Alexa and Key by Amazon In-Garage Delivery, supports two-way communication, and lets you mute the microphone using the myQ app.

9. Dehumidifier

During hot and humid days, your garage might accumulate moisture. Excess moisture can lead to wet garage walls and mold infestation. By using a dehumidifier, you can prevent moisture-related harm while also enhancing indoor air quality and comfort.

Portable dehumidifiers, like the Honeywell 50 Pint Energy Star Smart Dehumidifier, are suitable for various home spaces, including residential garages.

The dehumidifier has smooth gliding wheels that make moving it around easy and can remove up to 70 pints of moisture. It automatically detects garage humidity and activates dehumidification to remove it. Plus, it consumes less energy than traditional dehumidifiers, saving energy costs.

The smart dehumidifier also integrates with Alexa, allowing you to operate it from anywhere remotely. You can use it to protect walls, appliances, and furniture in up to 4,000 sq. ft. garages.

Smarten Your Garage for Security and Convenience

Garages act as secure havens for valuable belongings, including cars. Transforming your garage into a smart space can improve accessibility and security and potentially save money.

To make your garage smarter, consider adding smart garage gadgets, like smart door openers, lights, and cameras. These and other highlighted smart garage devices are just the tip of the iceberg. There are numerous lesser-known gadgets that can add a futuristic touch to your home.
