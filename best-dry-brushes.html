Frequently Asked Questions

Do dry brushes actually work?

Depending on your skin's tolerance level, dry brushing with a soft, medium, or stiff bristle brush and firm pressure can help slough off dead skin cells while encouraging lymphatic drainage.

What does dry brushing do?

Dry brushing can serve a number of benefits, from improving blood flow to exfoliating skin. "The practice is considered a physical or mechanical form of exfoliation, so it would be comparable to using a body scrub," says Marino. "It ultimately can improve circulation, cellulite appearance, and enhance moisture retention."

How many times a week should you dry brush?

Experts suggest dry brushing in the direction of the heart no more than once or twice weekly prior to showering to avoid potential skin irritation.
