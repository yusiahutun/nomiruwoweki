1. He has a stadium named after him

At 18 years of age, Eduardo Camavinga already has a . The stadium is in La Chapelle-Janson near his hometown and where he grew up in Fougères. Its name is Stade Intercommunal Eduardo Camavinga. Where is Camavinga from? He is originally from Congo because of his parent’s heritage but was born in Cabinda, Angola. He obtained French citizenship on 5 November 2019, making him eligible to play for the France national team.

3. He became France’s 2nd youngest player to score for the Les Bleus at 17 years 11 months

On 7 October 2020, Eduardo Camavinga started the game for France against Ukraine at the Stade de France. The French midfielder wasted no time in making an impact on his national team. In the first ten minutes of the match, Olivier Giroud connected with a cross and brought down the ball for Camavinga, who attacked the space.

The midfielder adjusted his body and managed a superb overhead kick to put the ball past Ukraine’s goalkeeper. After scoring his first goal at an international level, he became France’s second-youngest player ever to score for the Les Blues at 17 years 11 months. His opening goal also allowed France to go on a 7-1 rampage of Ukraine.
