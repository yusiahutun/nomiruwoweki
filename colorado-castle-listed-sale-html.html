A historic Colorado 'castle in the woods' has hit the property market for $27 million after being last bought in 1992 for $1.4 million.

The century-old Greystone Manor and Estate was completed in 1917 and Genevieve Chandler - a prominent Denver socialite - moved in and turned the vast estate into the site of elite parties and formal luncheons through the 1920s. 

Described as 'secluded mountain living at its best' - the sprawling property saw three owners after Chandler before being purchased by Richard and Pamela Bard in 1990.

Renowned as one of Colorado's true legacy properties - the estate features several properties including a Manor House, a Pavilion, the Guest House, the Carriage House, a Pool House, the Stone Cottage and the Log Cabin.

Richard Bard said he wanted a 'castle in the woods' - and the realtor he worked with told him he would love the Greystone Estate.

'We agreed to look at it, and as we drove up the driveway, I thought, ‘We’re going to buy this',' Bard told the Denver Post.

The pair lived in the Guest House for the first four years they spent at the stunning Colorado estate - while they renovated the 12,000 square foot Manor House. 

The breathtaking Manor House boasts six bedroom, a study overlooking the pool, an exercise room and a 'hidden security bedroom'.

The master suite showcases a marble jacuzzi-style bath tub and an oversized shower with an adjoining dressing area and walk-in closet with a skylight. 

Other features of the main property on the estate include a state of the art prep kitchen alongside a main kitchen, a library, a powder room and French doors that lead to an outside deck.

The Pavilion is described as 'an unforgettable place for entertaining' and offers 5,746 square feet and two floors of space for hosting. It was built in 2001 by the Bard couple for their daughter's wedding.

Its main room has striking 40 inch oak-beamed vaulted ceilings and full size glass doors and windows which showcase the stunning surrounding views of pine trees and mountains.

The quaint Guest House is described as 'a perfect place for visitors' - having been constructed as the 'servant's house,' the 3,100 square foot building now offers three bedrooms, a nursery/sitting room and two bathrooms.

Featuring beautiful oak wood floors and traditional wood-burning fireplaces - the Guest House will make visitors feel at home within the elegant and comforting vibes that the sweet building provides.

Still luxurious - the more humble building has an attached three car garage and four patio and porch areas that lead to the stunning Colorado outdoors.

Described as 'a place for summer leisure' - the Pool House was built originally in 1940 and remodeled in 1992 and 1999. It is a fully furnished self-sufficient apartment with a kitchen that faces the gorgeous rectangular blue pool that sits amidst the nature of Colorado's woodlands.

Lastly, the Log Cabin is an ode to the exclusive estate's modest beginnings. The historic shack was originally located on a Wyoming dude ranch - but was relocated to the Greystone Estate in the 1980s.

The Log Cabin is tucked away from the main buildings - offering a private spot to enjoy panoramic views and watch wildlife in the cozy and intimate environment. 

The Bards have now listed their historic one-of-a-kind property for $27 million.

Richard said: 'It’s had a lot of great uses for us over the years. What I love the most is the quiet. Get up every morning and go for a walk and hear nothing but birds and the occasional squirrel'
