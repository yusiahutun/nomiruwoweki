Professional Life and Career

Ichiro made his NPB debut on July 11, 1992, playing for Orix BlueWave. He made his first appearance in 1994 and played till 2000. During this, he played 130 game seasons and became the first player to top 200 hits in a single season. Similarly, he also set a PL record with a batting average of .385.

With his excellent playing, Ichiro was named “Hit Manufacturing Machine”. In 1998, Ichiro participated in a seven-game exhibition series between America and Japan. On April 2, 2001, Ichiro made his MLB debut by signing a 3-year contract with “Seattle Mariners”. This year was a fabulous year for Ichiro which marked his rising career.

In the 2004 season, Ichiro broke the record for the most number of hits with 262 hits in a season. Before, it was kept by George Sisler with 257 hits in 1920.

Ichiro was also a part of the Japanese National Team who won the World Baseball Classic in 2006. In July 2007, Ichiro extended his contract with the Mariners for 5 years. Likewise, he became the 2nd youngest player to gain 3000 top-level professional hits on July 29, 2008.

Furthermore, Ichiro became the first MLB player to gain a 200-hit mark for 10 seasons in a row. On July 23, 2012, he was sold to the “New York Yankees” by the “Mariners”. Moreover, Ichiro signed a one-year contract with “Miami Marlins” on January 23, 2015.

In 2019, Ichiro retired from baseball at the age of 47. He had an emotional farewell in Tokyo.
