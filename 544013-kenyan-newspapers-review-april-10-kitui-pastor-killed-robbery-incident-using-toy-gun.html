Kenyan Newspapers Review for April 10: Kitui Pastor Killed in Robbery Incident Was Using Toy Gun

 Posted on October 6, 2024  |  4 minutes  |  806 words  |  Larita Shotwell

On Wednesday, April 10, the local newspapers covered the indictment on the side of the Kenya Kwanza government, ranging from the fake fertiliser scandal, doctors' stand-off and the move by a host of senators to oppose the housing levy.

One of the publications covered a shocking incident in which a pastor was killed in a robbery incident.

1. The Standard

The Standard highlighted the incident in which a Kitui cleric was killed while undertaking robbery.

Pastor Ezekiel Muinde of the Heaven City Chapel in Kitui West had accompanied his accomplice to the Kavisuni market, where he was gunned down.

They attacked a businessman at his shop on the dusk of Sunday, April 7.

According to detectives, the duo cornered the businessman moments after he had closed shop, ordering him to give his phones and money.

The victim raised an alarm, attracting the police patrolling the centre.

Muinde and his accomplice were ordered by the police to surrender, but they would defy, with the cleric brandishing his toy pistol.

This prompted fire from one of the officers who shot and killed the pastor instantly, with his mate running for his dear life while being trailed by rounds, leaving behind their getaway motorcycle.

Police established that the accomplice was an assistant at the preacher's church.

The duo presented as police officers when they showed up at the businessman's shop.

2. Daily Nation

The daily reported on the misdoings of the Kenya Kwanza regime which has been riddled with challenges, among them the doctors' strike.

On Tuesday, Azimio La Umoja threatened to join the medics on the streets to demand their grievances be addressed by the state.

Wednesday, April 10, would be the 28th day of the industrial action.

Wiper leader Kalonzo Musyoka claimed over 500 people had died due to the ongoing strike, laying blame on the government.

"We are consulting, and if they do not stop the strike, obviously, Kenyans are free to join. Perhaps taking to the streets is the only language they understand," said Kalonzo.

The doctors are seeking redress in 19 issues, including the posting of interns, promotion, and the implementation of the 2017 Collective Bargaining Agreement (CBA), which outlines their remuneration.

But President William Ruto declined the remuneration demand stating that the government was broke and was striving to live within its means.

3. People Daily

People Daily spared space for the fake fertiliser saga which has seen ranking officials in government contradicting themselves.

The Kenya Bureau of Standards (KEBS) recalled several bags of substandard fertiliser under the government subsidy program.

But the officials in the government have been reading from different scripts while acknowledging the existence of the fake inputs.

Agriculture CS Mithika Linturi said only 3,000 bags had been confirmed to be fake.

On his part, Deputy President Rigathi Gachagua explained that over 50,000 bags had been flagged as fake.

This as Public Service CS Moses Kuria stated that 2,000 bags of fertiliser under the subsidy programme were confirmed to be fake.

President William Ruto while touring a National Cereals and Produce Board (NCPB) in Eldoret warned the cartels supplying farmers with substandard products.

He however refrained from divulging the number of bags of fertiliser confirmed as substandard.

Farmers decried being supplied with bags comprising stones and animal wastes disguised as fertiliser.

4. Taifa Leo

The Swahili newspaper reported on the ongoing trial of Edwin Chiloba's murder case.

An Eldoret court was told how the prime suspect in the murder of the LGBTQ activist used the latter's mobile number days after the death.

A prosecution witness narrated to the court how Jacktone Odhiambo used Chiloba's phone to pay for massage services barely 24 hours after his death.

The witness said she was introduced to Odhiambo by her friend, who said she had secured a client who needed massage and sexual services.

She informed Justice Reuben Nyakundi that she permitted her friend to provide the customer with her phone number as she was providing services for a fee as a means of earning.

The woman said Odhiambo arrived at her residence in a taxi and went to a nearby short-stay apartment where he had booked a room.

While at the Airbnb, the woman offered the suspect sexual and massage services, after which she was paid KSh 4,000 through the M-Pesa line of the slain LGBTQ model.
