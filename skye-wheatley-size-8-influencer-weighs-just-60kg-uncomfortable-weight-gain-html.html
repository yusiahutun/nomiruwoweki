Skye Wheatley: Size 8 influencer who weighs just 60kg is 'uncomfortable' with her weight gain

 Posted on August 25, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  621 words  |  Martina Birk

Skye Wheatley has expressed her discomfort at gaining several kilograms, despite only being a size 8.

The former Big Brother Australia star-turned-influencer, 28, raised eyebrows on Sunday after she complained on Instagram about the downsides of 'bulking', bodybuilding strategy which involves consuming excess calories to build muscle.

'I weigh 60kg (weighed myself at the gym the other night) and the lightest I've been is 54kg and the heaviest is 64kg,' the mother-of-two responded to a fan who asked about her recent weight gain.

'I know it's uncomfortable gaining weight. Like, I do feel pretty uncomfortable at the moment, but I'm just enjoying the junk in the trunk to be honest and it's only because my clothes are tight,' Skye explained.

The blonde said she recently bought size 8 jeans to accommodate her curvier shape, but insisted she embraces her new body because she 'can easily lose weight if she wants to'.

Her post ended with a preemptive response to fans who may criticise her for suggesting she's overweight at such a petite size. 

'I know size 8 is still small but I'm used to being so skinny,' she wrote, before pointing out that 'sometimes 1-3kg can make such a difference in how you feel within your body.' 

But this didn't stop Instagram 'snark ' account DutchMinty from calling out Skye in a furious rant about body-obsessed influencers hours later.

The anonymous admin slammed influencers for drawing draw attention to their slim physiques, often by announcing the size of their clothing or by sharing photos of themselves with noticeable 'thigh gaps'. 

'I couldn't believe my eyes when I read Skye Wheatley's Q&A tonight. Disappointed in herself for weighing 60kg (size 8),' the post read.

Skye has made no secret of her past struggles with body image, having recently admitted she once struggled with anorexia and binge eating. 

In a TikTok video, Skye explained that while she has been doing much better since becoming a mother, she still has bingeing episodes.

However, she stressed that she has not been diagnosed by a medical professional with a binge-eating disorder. 

'I have suffered with body image and how my body looks and didn't have a good relationship with food. I would binge eat to where I would just sleep... eat to the point of exhaustion,' she said. 

If you or anyone you know needs help treating an eating disorder, contact the Butterfly Foundation on 1800 33 4673 (1800 ED HOPE). 

What is a binge-eating disorder?  

According to Mayo Clinic, a binge-eating disorder 'is a serious eating disorder in which you frequently consume unusually large amounts of food and feel unable to stop eating'

Symptoms can include dieting without weight loss, feelings of guilt or depression, eating when you're not hungry, eating within a two-hour window
