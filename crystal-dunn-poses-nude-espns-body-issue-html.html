Crystal Dunn poses nude for ESPN's Body Issue

 Posted on August 4, 2024  |  5 minutes  |  941 words  |  Reinaldo Massengill

Crystal Dunn is the epitome of small but mighty. 

At just 5'2 she's competing at the top level as a forward for the United States Women's National Soccer Team and was the youngest player to win the National Women's Soccer League MVP and Golden Boot in 2015. 

But in an interview with ESPN The Magazine, for its annual Body Issue, the 25-year-old athlete revealed that she struggled with her height and revealed that it was a source of self-doubt when it came to her athletic ability. 

Doubt: Professional soccer player Crystal Dunn, 25, revealed that she struggles to accept her height and that it made her self-conscious 

'At first, it wasn't easy,' she said. 'In the early stages, when I realized I was going to be probably the shortest player on the teams I was on, it was hard. I felt like girls were getting recruited over me strictly because of their height, and it made me self-conscious.

'I had doubts about my height and size on the pitch. But I grew - I mean, I matured - and understood you can't have it all. I know tall people who want to be shorter. You want what you don't have. Once I understood that, I learned to take advantage of my body.'

The New York native had to find other ways to be successful in the sport where athletes such as Alex Morgan and Hope Solo are over 5'6. 

For starters, she worked on her lower body and made sure it was as strong as it could be along with accepting that she didn't necessarily have the same body as other girls.    

'I remember when I was young, before we started lifting and working out, I looked like I was bench-pressing other humans,' she said. 

'I looked different than other girls. I had to be okay with the fact that I had a strong physique, no matter if people looked at me in an accepting way or not.'

As a woman, Crystal admitted that she sometimes wants to hide her 'defined and muscular' legs because she doesn't want to look super strong.

New perspective: Crystal said she matured and was able to understand that 'you can't have it all' 

Self-love: Crystal has also come to accept her body even though a times, she wanted to hide 'defined and muscular' legs

But she has come to love her lower body partially because of Serena Williams who she sees as role model for not only ignoring the flack about her body but rising about it to become one of the most dominant athletes in the world.

'Watching her go through that makes me feel like I understand her. Being strong is beautiful, and that is what I have allowed myself to understand through her situation and that is how I feel about myself,' she said.   

In college, at the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill, Crystal played 'a crazy amount of positions' and as a result, was awarded the ACC offensive player of the year as a senior and the defensive player of the year as a junior. 

Of everything she ever won, those are two of her proudest accomplishments because it's hard to balance being a top goal scorer and a top defender. 

But as one of the only players that has managed to do both, Crystal said she's proud of her ability to be a 'diverse player' and use her body in different ways. 

Talent: With her plans to improve her upper body, Crystal is still extremely proud of all the things she has been able to accomplish and the future successes to come

Athletic: Crystal competing against China at FirstEnergy Stadium on June 12, 2018 in Cleveland, Ohio

She also noted the importance of not just being a great athlete on the field but making sure her body is physically healthy off the field as well.  

And that's why she's trying to improve her core and upper-body strength now. And even though she admires her lower body, (she said it has made her the player she is today), she's trying to be more body-conscious and create a strong upper body to go with it. 

But even with her plans to improve her upper body, Crystal is still extremely proud of all the things she has been able to accomplish and the future successes to come. 

'At 25, I accept who I am. I am not the biggest, but that doesn't mean I can't be the quickest or the smartest and find other ways of being successful,' she said. 

'I think in this [Body Issue] shoot, that is exactly what I want to represent: a strong figure. I want to capture the confidence I have and the power I possess.' 

Crystal played as a defender for the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill where she won an NCAA Women's Soccer Championship in 2012. 

That same year, she competed in the FIFA U-20 Women’s World Cup and helped her team win the Cup 1-0 against Germany in the final match, playing every minute of the tournament.

She competed in the 2016 Rio Olympics as a member of the US Olympic women's soccer team where and became the seventh US women's player with five goals in a game, joining Abby Wambach, Michelle Akers, Brandi Chastain, Amy Rodriguez, Sydney Leroux and Tiffeny Milbrett.

She is now a member of the US Women's National Soccer Team where she plays as forward alongside fellow teammates, Alex Morgan, Tobin Heath and Savannah McCaskill.
