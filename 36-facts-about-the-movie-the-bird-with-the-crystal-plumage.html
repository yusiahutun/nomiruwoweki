The Bird with the Crystal Plumage is a classic Italian thriller directed by Dario Argento and released in 1970. It marks Argento’s directorial debut and set the stage for his illustrious career in the horror genre. This gripping film follows an American writer, Sam Dalmas, who witnesses a brutal murder while visiting Rome. As he becomes tangled in the investigation, Sam soon realizes that he may be the next target of the mysterious killer. With its suspenseful plot, stunning visuals, and a haunting score by Ennio Morricone, The Bird with the Crystal Plumage paved the way for a new era of Italian giallo cinema.
