    How To Choose the Best Japanese Moisturizer?

    Here're a few tips to consider before you slide a Japanese moisturizer into your shopping cart. 

    Ingredients 

    Checking the ingredients is a rule to follow before buying a Japanese beauty product. Look for organic acids and natural ingredients like hyaluronate, plant extracts, skin-firming collagen, vitamins, glycerin, essential oils like rapeseed oil and olive oil, etc. These are excellent for your skin and improve its overall health. If you're allergic to a specific ingredient, stay away from products containing that. Also, your ideal pick shouldn't have harsh chemicals, as they can cause redness, irritation, and other skin problems. 

    Skin Type 

    You should always be aware of your skin type when choosing a moisturizer. If you have excessively dry or flaky skin, you may consider an intense hydrating moisturizer loaded with essential oils, collagen, or hyaluronic acid. For oily skin types, non-comedogenic formulas or those that don't cause breakouts can be a perfect fit. Sensitive skin types can go with hypoallergenic or non-irritating blends. Also, ensure that the product suits your skin and is gentle. You may go for a patch test to check your skin’s sensitivity to a particular ingredient. 

    Formula 

    Japanese face and body moisturizers are available in different formats, from creams to lotions and gels. Creams are ideal for normal, aging, and mature skin. Lotions have semi-thin consistency and work well on dehydrated, sensitive, and oily skin types, especially during the summer. Gels are usually perfect for oily and dried skin.

    FAQ's

    How many times should I moisturize my face?

    You can moisturize your face twice a day after cleansing on an everyday basis.

    Is it ok to use a serum with a Japanese moisturizer?

    Yes, you can use a serum and moisturizer simultaneously. First, apply a serum that targets your specific skin problem. Then follow up with a moisturizer.

    Are there any Japanese moisturizers with sun protection benefits?

    Yes, some moisturizers that include ingredients like antioxidants, Isoflavone, plant collagens, etc., may protect against UV damage.

    Are Japanese moisturizers cruelty-free and/or vegan?

    Most Japanese moisturizers do not contain animal-derived products. Instead, they contain natural ingredients.

    How long does it typically take to see results from using Japanese moisturizers?

    You can expect visible results within one to three months. But you've to be consistent with using the product.

    Can Japanese moisturizers be used under makeup?

    Of course, you can wear them under your makeup. This will hold the makeup layers tightly and avoid them from shedding.
