Self-care rituals with... Bobbi Brown

 Posted on February 7, 2024  |  6 minutes  |  1114 words  |  Aldo Pusey

Bobbi Brown is the first to admit that her approach to wellness isn't picture perfect, though it's forgiving and truly attainable nature is what makes it all the more admirable. "I try to make the best choices as much as possible, but not beat myself up when I do something that doesn't make me feel great," the make-up artist, entrepreneur and beauty founder admits candidly. "If I wake up in the morning feeling inflamed or puffy or tired, I stop and say, 'okay, what did I do yesterday? Did I not drink enough water?' I guess it would be called intuitive wellness. Listening to yourself."

If you're not familiar with Brown's established career, you may be familiar with the success of her first self-titled beauty line or latest make-up venture, Jones Road, named after a street near her Hamptons retreat. As a make-up artist, Jones has worked with the likes of Yasmeen Ghauri and Cindy Crawford and has authored many an esteemed guide in the realm of make-up application. It comes as no surprise, therefore, that her personal wellness journey has been contoured to fit her bustling career-focused lifestyle.

"I think it's important to realise that everyone is individual and what works for one person doesn't work for everyone," Brown says. "Everyone is on a different journey in their health and wellness. Where some people think 'I can’t drink', I really like a cocktail. But instead having a sugary one, I have either a vodka or tequila on the rocks with a lot of fresh lemon or lime juice."

Here, the 66-year-old make-up artist invites us into her tried and true wellness routine, from infrared saunas and breathwork to weight training and the expert self-care advice she's garnered throughout her career.

On the duality of her morning routine…

"I usually wake up to the sound of my dog, get up and immediately go into the kitchen to pour myself two glasses of water. I put either lemon and sea salt in it, or I take a greens powder. While I'm drinking it, I'm also make a double espresso, which I have with a tablespoon of half and half milk in it.

"My morning ritual is something I have tried to break; I usually walk with my coffee back to my bed and I open my iPad to read the newspaper. Sometimes, I go down a rabbit hole on Instagram. I do that, scare myself silly and then I jump up and I do exercise before I do anything else."

Courtesy of Jones Road Beauty

On the key to consistent exercise...

"If I'm walking, I will put on sunscreen, my headphones and just get out the door. I use Miracle Balm every day, too. I start with shade Tawny, which I apply all over my face as a tint. It adds a tint, but also moisture. When I want a little more brightness I layer Pinched Cheeks, which is a pinky rose. Truthfully, the inspiration for it was how I always pinched my cheeks to get the perfect colour blush."

"Normally, I call someone while I walk. It's the time I find myself the most peaceful to talk and not rushed or distracted. I usually call someone in the UK or I have a couple of girlfriends I know are up [at 7am] and could talk. Then I get my business calls out of the way. I usually walk for about an hour and fifteen minutes.

"Weight training is one of the things that has aided my wellness the most. I started and stopped a few times in my life and I notice such a big difference when I'm doing it. I sit up straighter, I feel better. My clothes look better. I love working with the trainer because then I have a place that I must get to. I do it religiously at 8 or 9am and then I'm set up for the rest of the day. I do it three times a week."

On her attainable evening routine…

"I wear Invisalign so I have to be really careful cleaning my teeth. I wash my face using the Jones Road Cleansing Stick; it is really a lazy girl's way to get ready for bed. You just put it on, splash water and take a cloth to remove everything. Then I put moisturiser on. That's been my time to do product development and I think I found a winner.

"At 8 or 9pm, I'll have a calming tea and I really love Celestial Seasonings Peppermint Tea. That is my go-to."

On her whole-food, high protein diet…

"I'm someone who has tried every single diet mentality. I really believe that what you eat is more important than what you put on your face. What works for me – because being extreme does not work and I always want what I cannot have – is making really good choices. Most of the time that just means that I really focus on food that grows in the ground and the best form of wholegrains I can find. I'm eating slower and being more mindful."

"I really believe what you eat is more important than what you put on your face"

"I've been adding more protein into my diet, too. The nutritionist I recently saw said she thought I wasn't having enough protein, so I've been adding it with a protein powder. My afternoon snack, because I'm too lazy to make a smoothie, is two scoops in a cup with hot water and a little bit of something creamy. I essentially make myself a hot cocoa."

Courtesy of Jones Road Beauty

On managing mindfulness with breathwork...

"Have you noticed that I did not mention meditation yet?! It is so hard, especially when you live such a busy life, you have a business and all of those things. It is not as easy to implicate as everyone says. What I have found that works for me is incorporating a breathing practice. Even if I'm in a meeting and I can see myself starting to be a little bit up, I just breathe and it calms everything down. A DJ in America called Durand said just take 60 seconds and just take a breath. It's like a mini massage.

"I split my time between a couple of different houses and one house has an infrared sauna. I find that it really helps with relaxation. I like sitting in a hot tub when I can, too. I'm also seeing a new a chiropractor who does kinesiology and he teaches me when you feel stressed or anxious to use visual techniques."
