Who is John Vernon Bevan Olyphant?

John Vernon Bevan Olyphant is a former executive and career man from the United States. He is famous for being the father of famous American actor Timothy Olyphant.

John was born on 25 April 1941 in New York City, USA and is 82 years old (as of 2023). His parents are John Kensett Olyphant and Adele Sloane Hammond. John is a descendant of the Vanderbilt family from New York, a prominent family in American history known for their success in various areas, including railroads and shipping.

John Olyphant attended boarding school in the East of the USA. He later accepted an athletic scholarship to the University of Arizona (U of A) in the fall of 1959 to study at the College of Mines. However, when exploration opportunities decreased, John switched to geological engineering and ultimately received his B.Sc. degree from the College of Agriculture.

Career

John Vernon Bevan Olyphant began his career after graduating from the University of Arizona. In 1964, he joined the US Army and served in Vietnam as a member of the 101st Airborne and with the Special Forces (Green Berets) as an "A" team leader, achieving the rank of Captain.

In 1970, Bevan was honourably discharged from the military and joined Del Monte Foods' International Division. While working for Del Monte Foods' Bevan toured various places, including Hawaii and the South Philippines.

In 1973, while in California for additional training, he received an offer from Gallo Wines, which he took. He served at Gallo Wines as the vice president of production for 17 years. In 1990, John retired from Gallo Wines and, seeking adventure, went to the South Pacific and worked as a black pearl farmer.

In 2000, he returned to southern Arizona to a family ranch in the Sonoita area to join the family cattle business.
