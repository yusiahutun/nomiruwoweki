Kenyans Irked by Man Who Told Safaricom How Lipa Mdogo Mdogo Phones Are Tampered With

 Posted on September 24, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  463 words  |  Larita Shotwell
  • A Facebook user claimed he knew how Kenyans were hacking Safaricom's Lipa Mdogo Mdogo phones
  • The man informed the company via social media while urging them to look for ways to stop the tampering with the devices
  • His sentiments did not impress many Kenyans, who expressed their displeasure in the comments section

A man has attracted the wrath of Kenyans after informing Safaricom how people tamper with their Lipa Mdogo Mdogo phones.

Kenyans hacking Lipa Mdogo Mdogo phones?

The service offers clients phones on loan and they must pay a certain amount daily. According to the Safaricom website, loanees pay manageable daily instalments of KSh 20.

The device is locked if the user fails to pay the daily fee and activated when they clear the amount.

On Wednesday, March 22, a Facebook user named Collins Sissey informed the telecommunication company how witty Kenyans had bypassed their security and were using the phones while defaulting on payment.

“Hello Safaricom PLC there are some phone fundis who are able to manipulate the Lipa mdogo mdogo phones by changing its motherboard so that owners can default payments,” his post read.

Collins urged Safaricom to upgrade their next of phones to fix the issue.

“So your new sets of brands, you must improve them so that they can't be tampered with. We must pay debts.”

Safaricom acknowledged the message and wrote back, saying.

“Good evening, Collins; we have shared your feedback. Thank you for the heads-up!”

Most Kenyans were unimpressed by the message, as others questioned whether changing the motherboard would work.

Steve Momo Cissoko:

“The way people are struggling with high price crisis, you still have the guts to expose your fellow Kenyans?”
