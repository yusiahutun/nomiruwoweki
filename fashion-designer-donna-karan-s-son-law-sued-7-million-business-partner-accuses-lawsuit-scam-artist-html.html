Donna Karan's son-in-law is being sued for $7m 'for being a scam artist'

 Posted on August 18, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  599 words  |  Reinaldo Massengill

The son-in-law of legendary fashion designer Donna Karan is being accused of being a scam artist in a new lawsuit filed against him. 

Gianpaolo de Felice, who is married to the designer's daughter Gabby Karan de Felice, is being sued for more than $7million by his business partner David Zara. 

The lawsuit charges that de Felice fleeced Zara in a private-jet business, denying him profits while bringing in a $420,000 salary and using company money to buy himself a $500,000 Ferrari, the New York Post reported.  

The company, Zen Air, flies celebrities all over the world to places including the Caribbean and California. 

Gianpaolo de Felice, who is married to Donna Karan's daughter, Gabby Karan de Felice, is being sued for more than $7million by his business partner, David Zara. Above the couple is pictured in New York this summer

Gianpaolo de Felice reportedly was the captain flying for his mother-in-law Donna Karan (above)

Zara owns 20 per cent of the company that was purchased in 2009, while de Felice controls the remaining 80 per cent, the Post reported. 

According to the lawsuit filed in Manhattan Supreme Court, de Felice ran the business but claimed it wasn't profitable and didn't pay Zara anything.

The former Alitalia pilot who once flew his mother-in-law's plane also allegedly lied to the IRS, claiming each partner took around $98,000 in distributions in 2009, the Post reported. 

However, de Felice took home the entire $196,000 the lawsuit alleges. 

According to the Post, de Felice purchased the luxury sports car with the company's money in 2011 and told the IRS that he spent 100 per cent of his time operating Zen Air, despite the fact he also operated restaurants in the Hamptons.  

In 2013, de Felice increased his salary when the business' revenues were down 20 percent, the lawsuit alleges.

David Zara (above) is suing Gianpaolo de Felice and accuses him of being a scam artist in the lawsuit

This fall, de Felice actually filed a lawsuit first against Zara in New Jersey in an attempt to remove him as his business partner. Donna Karen is pictured left and Gianpaolo de Felice along with his wife Gaby are on the right

'Ironically, it was de Felice who was adamant that no salaries be paid to him or Zara — period,' says the suit, according to the Post. 

This fall, de Felice actually filed a lawsuit first against Zara in New Jersey in an attempt to remove him as his business partner. 

In it, he claimed that Zara tried to steal clients away from the company for a competitor, the Post reported. 

Zara is reportedly broke and owes a number of creditors, including the IRS, close to $240,000.
