Mother of 11-year-old murdered after being abducted

 Posted on July 26, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  462 words  |  Larita Shotwell

Authorities say the mother of an 11-year-old Florida girl who was killed after being abducted in 2004, has died of a heroin overdose.

Polk County Sheriff Grady Judd said Thursday that Susan Schorpen's death is one of eight fatal overdoses being investigated in the central Florida county.

The Herald-Tribune reports the 47-year-old woman died April 10.

One week after 47-year-old Susan Schorpen died at a hospital in Winter Haven, the Polk County Sheriff's Office now says she 'ultimately died of a heroin overdose.'Pictured  in 2005

Schorpen was the mother of 11-year-old Carlie Brucia, far right, who went missing from a car wash in Sarasota in 2004. Brucia's body was found in a wooded area a few days later

The Sheriff's Office said Thursday that Schorpen is one of eight people believed to have died in the county in 2017 because of a heroin overdose. 

 Schorpen was taken to the hospital, according to the Sheriff's Office, and later died of an unknown cause. 

The Sheriff's Office said it does not conduct investigations into deaths that occur in the hospital, but Shorpen's family requested an investigation to determine whether it was drug-related after the family reportedly found drug materials at her home.

Schorpen struggled drug addiction, according to two people who attended Narcotics Anonymous meetings with her.

Schorpen was the mother of 11-year-old Carlie Brucia, who went missing from a car wash on Bee Ridge Road in Sarasota in 2004. Brucia's body was found in a wooded area days later

'Susan went downhill after the death of her daughter,' said Polk County Sheriff Grady Judd

They said the mother, and dog lover, was an 'unconditionally loving woman,' despite her problems.

“Susan went downhill after the death of her daughter,” said Polk County Sheriff Grady Judd at an inquest into her death.

Judd held up Schorpen’s photo as he talked about the increasing number of overdose deaths in the county.

“These people are users of illegal drugs and they are victims,” said Judd.

In the years following Brucia's death, Schorpen was arrested several times for drugs and prostitution.

Schorpen's pleas for the return of Carlie after she was kidnapped by Joseph P. Smith from a car wash captivated the nation during a six-day search in February 2004.

The girl's body was found in the woods behind a church less than three miles from where she was abducted. Smith is attempting to have his death sentence vacated. 

He was convicted of first-degree murder, sexual battery, and abduction.
