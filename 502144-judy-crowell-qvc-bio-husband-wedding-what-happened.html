Early life

Judy Crowell was born on 1 April in Tokyo, Japan. However, she grew up in many countries and states in the USA. Her father worked in the US Army, which forced Judy to move to places. She moved to at least 12 states while growing up.

Judy Crowell attended high school in Virginia, United States. But she did not finish high school in the US. Instead, her father moved to Germany and then to Belgium. Crowell eventually graduated from an international school in Belgium.

After graduating from high school, Judy Crowell enrolled at St. Lawrence University in 1977. She studied for two years and graduated in 1979 with a Bachelor's degree in Philosophy and Art History.

But Judy did not stop studying. Instead, she went to Florida State University, a public research university in Tallahassee, Florida, United States, in 1979 to study Communication and Media Studies. Eventually, Judy graduated in 1981.

In 2008, Judy Crowell returned to further her studies. She enrolled at the New England Fashion+Design Association institution. Judy studied Fashion Trend Forecaster, Fashion/Apparel Design.

Professional career

Judy Crowell began her professional career in 1981 after graduating college with a degree in communication. Her first job in the media industry was as a PM Magazine Host for WMTV NBC 15 in Madison, Wisconsin.

She left WMTV NBC 15 in 1984 and joined WZZM 13 in the Greater Grand Rapids, Michigan Area as PM Magazine Host. However, she worked for WZZM-13 for only one year.

In 1986, Judy Crowell started working as News Director for WLAV AM-FM Radio in Greater Grand Rapids, Michigan. However, she left the radio station in 1987.

In 1989, Judy Crowell was the on-air programme host for CVN in the Greater Minneapolis-St. Paul Area. But her stint at CVN was short because she left after five months.

In 1989, QVC hired Judy Crowell as the senior programme host. She worked in West Chester, Pennsylvania, hosting live remote broadcasts. Judy was the on-air host specialising in jewellery, fashion, fitness, home, beauty, and skincare.

Judy Crowell worked for QVC for 15 years. She left the network in 2004 and went to Tampa/St. Petersburg, Florida Area, to work for HSN.

At HSN, Judy Crowell worked as an on-air host. She was at the network for three years before leaving in 2008.

In 2008, Judy left Tampa/St. Petersburg and went to Knoxville, Tennessee. She joined JTV as a style and trend jewellery expert. Judy was also involved in brand development and product design for two new JTV Collections: Stratify and Blooming Jewels.

But after a year at JTV, Judy left the network and relocated to Minneapolis in 2014. She joined EVINE Live Inc. as the creator and designer of JOYA by Judy Crowell Jewelry. Judy worked for EVINE Live Inc. for four years until 2018.

Judy Crowell has been in two marriages, with one ending in divorce. However, it is undetermined whether she has children.

Judy Crowell's first husband was Jeffrey Hewson. Judy Crowell and Jeff Hewson exchanged vows in 1992. The two were colleagues at QVC when they met. Unfortunately, Hewson filed for divorce three months after the union.

After Jeff Hewson, Judy married Paul Deasy. Judy Crowell and Paul Deasy married in 2003. They have been together since their union and spend time traveling and hosting fitness instructions on Judy's YouTube channel.
