Darts fans completely bewildered as Mensur Suljovic starts leg with two 170s

 Posted on September 12, 2024  |  4 minutes  |  715 words  |  Aldo Pusey

Fishing at the start of a leg of darts isn't advised, but the entertaining Austrian Mensur Suljovic is, of course, the go-to player when it comes to chucking whacky darts.

The former Premier League player was missed from the World Championships in 2024 but reminded fans of exactly why he's so beloved during qualifying for the first European Tour event.

He's actually even attempted this sort of nine-dart pathway before and managed to pull it off in a Super League match a few years prior to this most recent go.

Suljovic absolutely loves a big fish, and any big checkout for that matter, as the memory of him stunning Michael van Gerwen into perplexity during the 2023 World Darts Championships with a stunning 161 checkout is fresh in the mind of darts fans.

That 161 is exactly what he likes to leave after two 170s, a move he's succeeded making before and tried again whilst attempting to qualify for the first Euro Tour event of 2024, something that has to be done due to controversial changes to the Euro Tour qualification set-up.

The top 16 players from the main Order of Merit, which doesn't include Luke Littler at the moment, automatically qualify for each of the Euro Tour events, along the with those on the ProTour Order of Merit out of those who haven't yet qualified.

The ProTour Order of Merit, currently headlined by Dave Chisnall, head into these first two events as the seeded players entering in the second round, with the other 32 drawn randomly in the first round.

It was against new tour card holder and Welshman Rhys Griffin, who was a surprise achiever of a card during Q School, that Mensur attempted the feat in a match that was won 6-3 by the Austrian number one.

Unfortunately for Mensur, a 105 was all he could muster, and he was eventually denied a place in the event by world semi-finalist Scott 'Shaggy' Williams who beat him 6-5 in a close encounter.

The 2018 Matchplay finalist will be a big miss from the first event of the Euro Tour, the Belgian Darts Open, which will feature Luke Littler after he came through the same round of qualifiers.
