KCB Given Green Light to Auction City Hall over KSh 4.3 Billion Debt

 Posted on March 8, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  492 words  |  Larita Shotwell

  • The now-defunct Nairobi City Council received the loan from Equity Bank, after which the Nairobi County inherited the debt, following the creation of devolved governments in 2013
  • This comes barely two months after Kenya Power cut power supply to City Hall Annex over unpaid bills running into millions

KCB Group is free to seize and auction assets of Nairobi county over an unpaid loan worth KSh 4.3 billion.

The High Court gave the bank the green light, with Justice Chacha Mwita dismissing the county's application challenging the amount awarded by an arbitrator in November 2019.

According to Business Daily, KCB went to court in 2020 to seek recognition of the award by Philip Bliss Aliker, who has picked by KCB and the Nairobi County government.

The now-defunct Nairobi City Council took over the loan from Equity Bank after which the Nairobi County inherited it, following the creation of devolved governments in 2013.

In September 2014, KCB acquired the loan from Equity, giving the county better terms on the facility. The terms included giving the county more time to repay the debt by increasing the maturity to eight years.

The judge said the law demands that the arbitral award be recognised as binding.

"City Hall runs a budget that is dominated by payment of salaries to its bloated workforce, leaving little for payment of debt and key projects," the publication noted.

The nod to auction City hall comes barely two months after Kenya Power cut power supply to City Hall Annex over unpaid bills running to millions.

TUKO.co.ke reported that the county government was sitting on KSh 1.5 billion owed to the utility firm.

The City Hall Annex offices generators powered the main City Hall, and the county assembly wing were plunged into darkness for over a week due to the disconnection. However, generators powered the main City Hall and the county assembly wing.

Kenya County Government Workers Union Nairobi Staff Branch Secretary Festus Ngari said the power cut had paralysed certain critical services at City Hall, forcing several workers not to report to work.

"The physically challenged and elderly staff are at a great disadvantage since they have to use stairs to access their offices as the lifts are not working," said Ngari.
