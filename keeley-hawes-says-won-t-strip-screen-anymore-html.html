The actress suggested she was too old to strip off, saying that the response would be: 'That's what a 41-year-old's boobs really look like?'

She explained the reasons were 'for me, for the kids, for my husband - not that he cares about all that - and for the viewer', saying: 'I think I've just gone past the stage where it's doable.'

Keeley - who has previously stripped off in television series Tipping The Velvet and film Complicity - added: '[T]here would be comparisons with things I did when I was in my 20s, which, as I say, I really don't need in my life.'

 Racy: She has previously stripped off for BBC series Tipping The Velvet with co-star Rachael Stirling in 2002 (above)

Steamy: However speaking to The Telegraph's Stella magazine, the mother-of-three said after performing past saucy scenes (above): 'Oh, I'm never going nude again. Never, ever, ever'

The Durrells star also revealed in the interview that her actor husband Matthew Macfadyen is paid more than her, even though they are considered as being on the same level in the television industry.

The pair married in 2004 and have two children together Maggie, 12, and Ralph, 10, as well as son Myles, 16, from Keeley's first marriage to Spencer McCallum.  

Speaking about their relationship on The Graham Norton Show on Friday, Keeley said: 'We never talk about work at home. I think that would be really boring and a bit weird. We talk about the children and cooking supper.' 

Wilder days: Keeley told an embarrassing story involving a very awkward 'walk of shame' during an appearance on The Graham Norton Show on Friday

She also revealed a rather embarrassing story from her wilder younger days, saying: 'Putting it delicately, I had a sleep over with someone and when I woke in the morning I made my exit only to find myself locked between the flat and the door. 

'I had to call through the letterbox to an old lady who then called the fire brigade. They then had to break in through a window and carry me down a ladder. 

'Talk about a walk of shame, it was quite something!'
