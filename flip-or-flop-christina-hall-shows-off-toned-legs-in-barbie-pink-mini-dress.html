Flip or Flop's Christina Hall shows off toned legs in Barbie pink mini dress

 Posted on May 27, 2024  |  2 minutes  |  401 words  |  Jenniffer Sheldon

Christina will star in the upcoming HGTV series Barbie Dreamhouse Challenge.

    Come on Christina, let's go party! Christina Hall was a vision in Barbie pink on Friday April 14 as she joined her castmates for the upcoming HGTV serie. 

    The mom-of-three looked incredible in a hot pink sequin mini dress that showed off her toned legs. The dress also featured flared sleeves and she accessorized with dark blue nails; she kept her long blonde hair loosely curled.

     Christina recently returned from vacation and is looking fabulous and sun-kissed in the picture; a second video from the glam room showed her sporting a natural makeup look. The 39-year-old  posed for the picture with Loren Ruch, Head of content at HGTV, and it was taken on set.

     The new series will comprise "four nostalgia-filled episodes" and feature eight teams of HGTV superstars "as they transform a Southern California home into a real-life Barbie Dreamhouse" finishing up with one Barbie fan getting the once-in-a-lifetime stay in the home. Alongside Christina and her co-star James Bender will be  Egypt Sherrod and Mike Jackson (Married to Real Estate); Jasmine Roth (Help! I Wrecked My House) and Antonia Lofaso (Beachside Brawl); Ty Pennington (Rock the Block) and Alison Victoria (Windy City Rehab); Jonathan Knight and Kristina Crestin (Farmhouse Fixer); Keith Bynum and Evan Thomas (Bargain Block); Brian and Mika Kleinschmidt (30 Day Dream Home); and Michel Smith Boyd and Anthony Elle Williams (Luxe for Less). 

    Each team will be given a room and instructions to design with a specific Barbie era including "1960s atomic age kitchen, a ‘70s disco vibe for Ken’s Den and a main bedroom decked out in ‘80s glam". 

    The show's announcement comes ahead of the July 21 release of Margot Robbie and Ryan Gosling's new movie, about which very little is known. 

    The film's Wikipedia reads: "After being expelled from Barbieland for being a less than perfect-looking doll, Barbie sets off for the human world to find true happiness," while the press release adds: "To live in Barbie Land is to be a perfect being in a perfect place.  Unless you have a full-on existential crisis.  Or you’re a Ken."
