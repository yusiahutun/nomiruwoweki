3 ways FOPEs Aria collection can add a touch of Italian glamour to your everyday wardrobe

 Posted on July 22, 2024  |  4 minutes  |  684 words  |  Larita Shotwell

The last few years have seen a subtle yet seismic shift when it comes to the way we wear jewellery. Timeless pieces with a modern aesthetic have been foregrounded thanks to the unprecedented popularity of the luxe athleisure trend. Investment jewellery that’s playful, fresh and elegant is the focus — whether you’re working a runway-ready look or searching for inspired pieces that have genuine style longevity.

If you’re looking for some fine jewellery to add a hint of Italian glamour to your wardrobe, heritage brand FOPE’s new Aria collection is subtle yet startling in its simplicity. The collection showcases elegant, lightweight pieces made from delicate, fine gold mesh chain. Think: lariat necklaces and earrings in yellow, white and rose gold, or cylinders set with white or black diamonds — all expertly designed to be layered up or worn as one stunning statement piece.

Nearly a century old, FOPE began life as a goldsmith’s workshop in Italy, before becoming a cutting-edge jewellery manufacturer in the 1970s. This led to the brand’s signature Novecento chain mesh design, as well as its groundbreaking Flex’it technology, whereby bracelets and rings are crafted with clever tech (an internal system of intricate solid gold springs) that give the pieces elasticity and allows them to flex and stretch with the wearer.

If you’re searching for exquisite, quintessentially Italian jewellery to elevate your everyday wardrobe, look no further.

1. Mix your metals

FOPE

Gone are the days when it was considered a veritable faux pas to mix your metal jewellery shades; it’s now a hallmark of fresh elegance and an easy but effective way of adding some edge to an outfit — something stylish Italian women have been doing for decades.

HOW TO STYLE IT

For a subtle look, layer bracelets and necklaces in an odd number. Sandwich two white gold necklaces of varying lengths with a rose gold one (both in the middle, or for a modern vibe, opt for a pair of FOPE Aria pendant earrings in rose gold, worn with your favourite yellow gold FOPE Essential Flex’it rings and bracelets. FOPE's Aria lariat necklace looks even more chic when layered with the long necklace with diamonds (both in yellow gold), and the white gold and black diamond athleisure-inspired chain from the same collection.

2. Opt for comfort

FOPE Rose gold necklace with a white diamond, £1,555

FOPE Rose gold necklace with a white diamond, £1,555

Blame the pandemic and its endless lockdowns, but somehow even the most dedicated devotees of high-octane glamour are now craving comfort without compromising on style. Take a tip from the Italian fashion editors and go for lightweight, comfortable pieces of jewellery that can be easily dressed up or down.

HOW TO STYLE IT

To channel the athleisure trend, try the shorter length (37mm) FOPE Aria chain necklace with a single white diamond, worn alone with a pair of Aria pendant earrings that have a smattering of diamonds at the stud. Nonchalant and thoroughly contemporary — it works just as well with your fine-knit cashmere crop top and leather trench at the weekend as it does with your more classic 9-5 wardrobe. As every Italian style goddess knows, the devil really is in the detail.

3. Wear it both ways

FOPE

Whether you’re shopping for a “treasure-forever” piece or just feeling inspired to shake up your jewellery wardrobe, so-called shapeshifting jewellery is the latest Italian street-style trend to have caught our eye. In other words, pieces that work more than one way — from adjustable lariats to earrings that transform their shape — giving us maximum two-way appeal.

HOW TO STYLE IT

FOPE's Aria Interchangeable earrings (made from fine mesh 18ct gold) come in three metals — rose, white and yellow gold — and thanks to their lightweight design, they’re super-comfortable, however you choose to style them. Wear the chains long for a playful look, up the elegance by shortening (loop chains from back to front) or simply remove altogether for a simple, low-key luxe stud design.
