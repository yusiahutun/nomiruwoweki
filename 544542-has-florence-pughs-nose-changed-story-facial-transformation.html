Who is Florence Pugh?

Florence Pugh was born on January 3, 1996, in Oxford to Deborah and Clinton Pugh. She is an English actress who made her acting debut in the 2014 TV drama film The Falling. Elizabeth gained recognition in 2016 for her leading role in the independent drama Lady Macbeth, winning the prestigious British Independent Film Award.

In 2019, Pugh had an international breakthrough with her roles in Fighting with My Family, Midsommar, and Little Women. This year, she received nominations for a BAFTA Award and an Academy Award. She was also awarded the Trophee Chopard at the 2019 Cannes Film Festival.

Florence has had roles in several other award-winning TV shows and series, including Black Widow, Don't Worry Darling, The Wonder, Oppenheimer, and Dune: Part Two. She voiced Goldilocks in the animation film Puss in Boots: The Last Wish.

Was Florence Pugh asked to change her appearance?

In the October 2022 interview with The Telegraph, Florence Pugh opened up about when she was advised to alter her face and eyebrows and lose weight in her early acting career at 19. She said,

“All the things that they were trying to change about me — whether it was my weight, my look, the shape of my face, the shape of my eyebrows — that was so not what I wanted to do, or the industry I wanted to work in. "I’d thought the film business would be like [my experience of making] The Falling, but actually, this was what the top of the game looked like, and I felt I’d made a massive mistake.”
