Bleach London has launched a digital hair salon

 Posted on April 19, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  513 words  |  Jenniffer Sheldon

Your ability to get a haircut might not be the most pressing point on your agenda right now but with hair salons, alongside other beauty technicians, having to close due to the coronavirus pandemic, how we look after our hair is going to have to change.

Luckily, Bleach London has announced an innovative way to help guide us through the often tricky transition to at-home hair maintenance with the launch of its virtual 'Hair Party' series, utilising the expertise of its brilliant stylists.

Designed to operate like a 'digital salon', it will focus on giving guidance to those who no longer have access to professional hair colour touch-ups, meaning bottle blondes can ask about regrowth and rich brunettes can receive advice on limiting their colour fading.

The hair salon chain, which has three locations in the capital that are currently closed due to the lockdown, is known for its bold hair colour transformations.

The first live conversation will be hosted by Bleach London's creative director Alex Brownsell, who will be sharing step-by-step guides on how to perfect your colour at-home. From natural shades to more conversation-starting hues, it's perfect for those who want to make an educated experiment with their hair during their extended time at home.

A host of celebrity clients, including Georgia Jagger and Pixie Geldof, are also set to exclusively share their hair stories, making the event informative and entertaining even for those who feel confident looking after their own tresses.

The first 'party' is due to take place sometime this weekend and to gain access customers simply need to purchase a product from the Bleach London site.

The date, time and virtual location will be kept a secret, with pass-holders being informed of the details by email, as part of a very special VIP service.

The events will then be held regularly, hosted by the salon's top 20 stylists, focusing on individual hair needs and colour choices - from dye damage to the perfect balayage. It will utilise the talent of the salon's staff who themselves are facing the uncertainty of little work while we are all on lockdown.

For those unable to 'attend' one of the live salon events, Bleach London is enhancing the 'live-chat' feature available on its website too.

Using the brand's colour families (bleach, vivid, pastel etc.) as a guide, individuals will be asked to explain their existing hair type and history, before being advised on the right products for their hair needs.

Other brands are trying to help too. For example, Hari's new Hair Helpline allows customers to continue to have access to the salon's talented team via a 15 minute virtual video consultation, using Whatsapp Video. Significantly, individuals can 'book in' with their regular colourist or stylist to talk about issues from disguising outgrown roots to mastering certain hairstyles - continuing the relationship they had before the lockdown.
