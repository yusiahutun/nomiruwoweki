Meaning of No Ideas Original by Nas

 Posted on June 14, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  494 words  |  Aldo Pusey

"No Idea's Original" by Nas is a thought-provoking song that explores the idea that there is nothing new under the sun. The overall theme of the song revolves around the notion that it's not what you do that matters, but how you do it. Nas delves into various aspects of life, such as happiness, materialism, women, and success, and challenges the listener to rethink their perspectives. The lyrics shed light on different themes, each contributing to the overarching message of the song.One standout lyric in the song is "If niggas could look inside my mind, you'll find where bodies are buried." This lyric introduces the theme of internal struggles and the burdens we all carry. Nas suggests that everyone has their own secrets and skeletons in the closet, hidden deep within their thoughts. It serves as a reminder that no one is perfect, and we all have our inner demons to battle. This theme also emphasizes that our personal journeys shape who we are and how we navigate the world.Another noteworthy line is "My tongue is power, it thrills women, kills demons." This lyric explores the power of words and the impact they can have. Nas suggests that the way we communicate and express ourselves can both uplift and harm others. It delves into the responsibility we have when using our words and the importance of using our tongues wisely. This theme connects to the idea that our actions and intentions matter more than what we actually do.The line "Your part of the world might be like colors and gangs, while on my side, brothers'll murder for different things" delves into the theme of cultural differences and individual motivations. Nas contrasts different perspectives and experiences, highlighting that what may seem significant to one person might not hold the same importance to another. This theme emphasizes the diversity and complexity of humanity, reminding us that our individual paths shape our priorities and values.Furthermore, the lyric "Watchin' for paint chips, don't want no lead in yo' child, but them gangstas put lead in yo' child" tackles the topic of systemic issues and the cycle of violence. Nas sheds light on the everyday struggles faced by those in marginalized communities, where the concern for basic safety conflicts with external influences that perpetuate harm. This theme reflects on the harsh realities that individuals in certain environments face, and the ongoing battle to protect loved ones and break free from the chains of circumstance.In conclusion, "No Idea's Original" by Nas covers a range of themes that all tie back to the overarching message of the song. Each standout lyric explores different aspects of life and challenges listeners to consider their perspectives. From internal struggles to the power of words, from cultural diversity to the cycle of violence, the song acts as a mirror, encouraging introspection and reflection. Ultimately, Nas reminds us that it's not what we do, but how we navigate and confront the world that truly matters.
