John was born in 1925 in the small Southern Illinois town of Eldorado. His parents were Peck and Noma Cavaness, and he was the only child in the family.

John's father trained him to stand for himself and tackle his challenges by himself, which was a great life lesson for the young John. Such traits made him competitive and successful in virtually everything he attempted.

Dr John's small size did not deter him from taking on significant challenges. He excelled in football and proved to be strong in physical fights.

The doctor was fortunate enough to come from a wealthy family that wasn't affected by the depression. Therefore, his parents had sufficient money to take him to high school and college. They also did not have any problem taking him to medical school, where he earned his medical skills.

Dr John was part of the United States Army (joined after completing high school) and took part in World War II. After leaving the army (honourably discharged), he got an opportunity to study medicine at the Washington University School of Medicine in St. Louis.

However, after coming back home from medical practice, he was abandoned by his first wife, Helen Jean. He found solace with a friend (Mariann Newberry), a nurse at Barnes Jewish Hospital. They married on October 3, 1952.

The doctor was abusive in the marriage and had several domestic violence situations. Nevertheless, they had their first child, Mark, in 1954, and the second, Kevin Dale, followed in 1956.

Dr John created a strong reputation in his town as he treated people in impoverished communities. The doctor and his wife had their third son, Dean Dale Cavaness, in 1962.

Despite John's infidelity, Mariann remained married to him. She even gave birth to another third child, Patrick Dale Cavaness, in 1966.
