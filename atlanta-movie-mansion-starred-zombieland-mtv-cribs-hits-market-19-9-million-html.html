Atlanta's most expensive home - which has featured in movies including Zombieland and The Three Stooges - has hit the market for a staggering $19.9 million.

The 40,000-square-foot movie mansion in the city's exclusive Buckhead neighborhood has also starred in television shows including MTV's 'Teen Cribs' and HGTV's 'Million Dollar Rooms'.

And it's easy to see why this home has caught the attention of location managers and audiences as its nine bedrooms, 15 bathrooms and dramatic driveway and grounds are dripping with grandeur.

Lavish: A 40,000-square-foot mansion that has featured in multiple movies has hit the market for $19.9 million

Palatial: The magnificent staircase may be recognizable from MTV's Cribs or the 2009 film, Zombieland

Tour: Jamen and Katelin Najjar, the children of owner Lee Najjar, give a tour on MTV's 'Teen Cribs' in 2009

The mansion is owned by Real Housewives of Atlanta's Kim Zolciak's former boyfriend, real estate mogul Lee Najjar. His son and daughter have previously shown off the home on MTV's Teen Cribs.

There is no expense spared at his lavish estate, which features a music studio, a hair and nail salon, a home theater, a ballroom, a cigar room, a wine cellar, two gyms and a wellness center.

The high crafted ceilings - many in 24-karat gold leaf - and hardwood and stone floors line expansive rooms. There are also seven kitchens and servants' quarters, as well as a separate guest house, according to the listing.

The opulence continues outside across three acres, with an enormous courtyard pool decorated with mosaics and surrounded with loungers, fountains and spas.

Expansive: The house, which was once listed for $25 million, has seven kitchens and servants' quarters

Grand: Some of the high ceilings, such as in the dining room, are decorated with 24-karat gold leaf

Spacious: The house in Atlanta's exclusive Buckhead nieghborhood has nine bedrooms and 15 bathrooms

No expense spared: As well as the bathrooms, the home also has steam rooms, wet rooms and spas

Elegant: Owners have included Big Poppa, the boyfriend of Kim Zolciak on Real Housewives of Atlanta

This pool area has detail unlike any other; European artisans were brought in to lay the stonework around the pool, at a cost of more than $1 million.

The extensive home, which also contains three fireplaces, was built in 2008 but retains a 1912 facade.

The home, which is in the ninth wealthiest zip code in the U.S., is also protected by gates, allowing residents and guests to enjoy their the spa visits, pool laps or recording sessions in peace.

It featured in Zombieland, a 2009 zombie comedy film starring Woody Harrelson, Jesse Eisenberg, Emma Stone and Abigail Breslin.

Outside: An enormous courtyard pool is decorated with mosaics and surrounded with fountains and spas

Perfect for entertaining: Cathedral-esque ceilings offer some shade on one of the home's balconies

Immaculate: European artisans were brought in to lay the $1 million stonework around the pool

Outdoor dining: The home, which has an outdoor kitchen, is in the ninth wealthiest zip code in the U.S.

The film's characters are trying to evade zombies as the travel across the U.S. and they head to the home for some reprieve. In the movie, it is the Hollywood, California mansion of Bill Murray.

It was also featured in the 2012 version of The Three Stooges, which starred Sean Hayes, Chris Diamantopoulos and Will Sasso.

And it also starred in 'Devious Maids', a drama produced by Eva Longoria.

Viewers were given a tour of the home by siblings Jamen and Katelin Najjar on Teen Cribs in 2009; they are the children of owner Lee Najjar.

On screen: The massive mansion featured in the 2012 version of The Three Stooges, starring (left to right) Sean Hayes as Larry, Chris Diamantopoulos as Moe and Will Sasso as Curly

Famous: It was also featured in Zombieland, starring Woody Harrelson (right) and Jesse Eisenberg

Guides: Jamen and Katelin Najjar show the cameras the pool area on Teen Cribs, explaining that they often fill up the home with their parties

Owner: Real-estate mogul Lee Najjar has put the mansion up for sale. It is Atlanta's most expensive home
