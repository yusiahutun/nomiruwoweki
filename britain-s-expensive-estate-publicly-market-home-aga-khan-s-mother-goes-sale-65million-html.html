Britain's most expensive estate ever on the market Hackwood Park House goes up for sale

 Posted on February 29, 2024  |  5 minutes  |  951 words  |  Aldo Pusey

A 24-bedroom mansion is set to become the most expensive country estate ever sold publicly after it was put on the market with a guide price of more than £65 million.

The Hackwood Park estate, near Basingstoke in Hampshire, is so exclusive potential buyers will have to prove their financial status and sign a confidentiality agreement before viewing.

Built in 1680, the main house has 24 bedrooms and 20 bathrooms and is more than 50 times the size of the average family home.

The main entrance is flanked by two substantial gatehouses and the 260 acres of grounds include a stunning botanical garden.

Hackwood House was previously owned by publishing tycoon Viscount Camrose - William Berry - who loaned it to the Royal Canadian Army to use as a hospital during World War Two.

More than 16,500 troops were treated on the estate and, when the war was over, it was returned to Viscount Camrose, who died in 1954.

The second Viscount Camrose, John Berry, owned the home until his death in 1995 when it was passed into the hands of his wife, Lady Camrose, mother of the Aga Khan. 

According to the electoral role, Lady Camrose was the last tenant at the mansion until her death in 1997.

Land Registry documents state the house was last sold between 1999 and 2003 for £14.5 million.

Its current owner, rumoured to be a foreign billionaire, bought the house through a British Virgin Island-registered company.

Over the past decade the estate has been impeccably maintained by its wealthy owner, who has carried out a substantial restoration of the main home.

It is now on the market with a strict 'price on application', with both Savills and Sotheby's refusing to reveal what it can be bought for.

The Hackwood Park estate is so exclusive potential buyers will have to prove their financial status and sign a confidentiality agreement before viewing

However, it is believed to be on the market for in excess of £65 million - making it the most expensive home currently for sale publicly in the UK.

Potential buyers who want to view the property may be required to 'provide references and evidence of their financial standing' before the agents agree to it. 

The selling agents say Hackwood House has been configured for a 'life with service', with some of the staircases and rooms allowing staff to discreetly pass between family and entertaining areas.

It also has a vast Grade II listed stable and coachhouse as well as four cottages. There is a deer park and Grade I listed ancient woodland.

Savills and Sotheby's Realty add that Hackwood Park tells the story of 'the history of England'.

The brief description states: 'Through its incarnation from Royal deer park, to hunting lodge, to grand aristocratic residence welcoming great political characters.

'It certainly has been touched by greatness throughout its many years.' 

The second most expensive country home currently on the market is Woolmers Park, the former home of the Queen's grandparents, which has an asking price of £30 million.

Just one house outside London is openly known to have sold for more than Hackwood House asking price. Park Place, near Henley-on-Thames, which went for a rumoured £140 million in 2011. However, it was sold on the grey market - where elusive properties go up for sale without being advertised or promoted.

Aga Khan is a British business magnate, racehorse owner and breeder. He is the 49th and current Imam of Nizari Ismailism, a denomination within Shia Islam.  

His Highness the Aga Khan became Imam - or spiritual leader - of the Shia Imami Ismaili Muslims on 11 July 1957 at the age of 20, succeeding his grandfather, Sir Sultan Mahomed Shah Aga Khan. 

He is the 49th hereditary Imam of the Shia Imami Ismaili Muslims and a direct descendant of the Prophet Muhammad through his cousin and son-in-law, Ali, the first Imam, and his wife Fatima, the Prophet's daughter.

The son of Prince Aly Khan and Princess Tajuddawlah Aly Khan - or Lady Camrose - the Aga Khan was born on 13 December 1936, in Geneva. 

He spent his early childhood in Nairobi, Kenya, and then attended Le Rosey School in Switzerland for nine years. He graduated from Harvard University in 1959 with a BA Honors Degree in Islamic history.
