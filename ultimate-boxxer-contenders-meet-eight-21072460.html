Ultimate Boxxer contenders: Meet the eight fighters battling for 50k prize

 Posted on May 16, 2024  |  3 minutes  |  636 words  |  Jenniffer Sheldon

Ultimate Boxxer returns this Friday as the heavyweights get their turn in the quick-fire knockout-laden tournament.

This event will be held at Planet Ice Arena in Manchester with a prize pot of £50,000 on offer.

The eight-man tournament will fighters battle it out over 3x3minute rounds in quarter-finals, semi-finals and a final.

Backed by famous names such as Amir Khan , Paulie Malignaggi and Anthony Crolla, the competition is growing in popularity.

The previous tournaments have produced plenty of knockouts and drama while music acts appear between bouts to keep fans entertained.

The real action is in the ring and Starsport takes a look at all the fighters taking part in this week’s tournament.

ULTIMATE BOXXER CONTENDERS

JONATHAN PALATA

(7 wins, 0 defeats)

Promoted by Frank Warren, he enters the fray with an impressive and unbeaten professional record, following a stellar amateur career which saw him rack up an ABA Novice Championship, London ABA Championship and he also won the prestigious Haringey Box Cup. He fights out of the famous Peacock Gym in Canning Town. Palata has fast hands and big reputation which sees him favourite to take the top prize.

NICK WEBB

(13 wins, 2 defeats)

A well-known name in the heavyweight scene, he he 11 knockout wins against opponents such as Ferenc Zsalek and Chris Healey. He has been in line to face the likes of Daniel Dubois and Nathan Gorman before they fell through, and will be hoping to put his only defeats to date against Dave Allen and Kamil Sokolowski behind him.

JAY MCFARLANE

(10 wins, 4 defeats)

The man with the most amazing story in the competition is the Glasgow hard nut. Grew up in a Caribbean orphanage before ending up in Scotland. McFarlane has fought on a Gennady Golovkin undercard at Madison Square Garden but had to sleep on a hotel floor before his fight. Will be desperate to pull it off.

KAMIL SOKOLOWSKI

(8 wins, 15 defeats)

Once a journeyman, now looking to be a contender. The Polish born Sokolowski has shared the ring with Dillian Whyte, Kash Ali and Gary Cornish. Has only been stopped twice and has been improving considerably under new trainer Gary Lane. A dark horse.

CHRIS HEALEY

(8 wins, 6 defeats)

The hometown favourite for this show, Healey is out to prove major back surgery will help him get back on track in his career. Hasn’t had it easy the last few years with the loss of his father in 2016 but now hoping to be able to focus on his career.

MARK BENNETT

(5 wins, 0 defeats)

A former army man who toured Afghanistan before a serious injury forced a medical discharge who will be looking to soldier through to the final. Bennett took up boxing as a 27-year-old and hasn’t lost a bout at amateur level or amongst the professional ranks. A dark horse.

JOSH SANDLAND

(4 wins, 1 draw, 1 defeat)

A former rugby league ace turned fighter, Sandland is hoping to make the big time in another sport. The ex-Huddersfield Giants and Wakefield Wildcats player has only lost once as a professional and hopes he can become more than a journeyman.

DANNY WHITAKER

(2 wins, 0 defeats)

The Yorkshireman was a white collar boxer who fancies his chances in the real world of professional boxing. A late replacement for Sean Turner in the competition, he will be up against it but fancies a few upsets.
