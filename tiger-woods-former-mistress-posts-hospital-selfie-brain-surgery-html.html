A MALFORMATION THAT FORCES THE BRAIN INTO THE SPINAL CORD 

A Chiari malformation is when the lower parts of the brain have been pushed down towards the spinal cord.   

There are three types, which differ in terms of seriousness and symptoms. 

Typical signs to look out include headaches, neck pain, dizziness, numbness, hearing loss and blurred vision.

Surgery is usually the best form of treatment. Sufferers can opt for decompression surgery - when doctors remove a small piece of bone from the base of the skull - or a spinal laminectomy - the removal of a piece of bone from the top of the spine.

Most people find their symptoms improve after the surgery, but sometimes it can lead to no improvements.  

It is thought around 1 in 1,000 people suffer from the malformation.
