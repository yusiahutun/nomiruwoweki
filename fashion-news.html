The celebrity-approved Ritz Paris merch is back

 Posted on March 17, 2024  |  2 minutes  |  348 words  |  Jenniffer Sheldon

The Ritz Paris merch loved by Hailey Bieber and Naomi Campbell is back. The collection – which is made in collaboration with Frame – is returning for its third drop this November, and now features more ready-to-wear pieces including bombers, blazers and wide-legged trousers.

"Taking the identity of a luxury hotel and creating a collection was a little unconventional when we started this collaboration three years ago," Frame co-founder Erik Torstensso said of when they first teamed up with the Ritz Paris. "We thought it would be a cult project for our friends, but the commercial success was unprecedented."

Frame x Ritz Paris

Related Story

First launched in October 2021, the capsule – which featured baseball caps, sweatshirts and trainers – sold out almost immediately, and was spotted on supermodels and influencers everywhere. Originally created to be a blend of American classics and French sophistication, the new collection takes this a step further with minimalist capsule classics.

"Part three is a much more elevated look at wardrobe staples in the Frame x Ritz Paris aesthetic," he adds. "From the quilted leather bomber to the most luxurious slippers, each piece is refined enough to transport you straight to a suite at Place Vendome, no reservation necessary."

Frame x Ritz Paris

In the drop, you will find cashmere cardigans and sweaters, velvet slippers, silky pyjama sets, swimsuits and more, all adorned with the legendary hotelier’s famed crest. Meanwhile, there will also be some classics returning from the previous two collections, including the baseball cap and the women's sweater.

"This new capsule collection is a testament to the incredible success of our collaboration with Frame. It combines the timeless elegance of the Ritz Paris with the contemporary, high-end style of this renowned American brand. A chic yet casual capsule that will once again appeal to discerning fashion enthusiasts."
