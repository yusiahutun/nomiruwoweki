The 11 Best Setting Sprays of 2024

 Posted on June 18, 2024  |  15 minutes  |  3122 words  |  Aldo Pusey

After spending extra time blending your foundation, meticulously lining your waterline, and carefully tracing your lips, the last thing you need is for the weather (or your nerves) to cause a makeup meltdown. Luckily, there's a foolproof way to keep your makeup from dripping off of your face, and it requires only one extra step at the end of your makeup routine. A quick spritz of a setting spray is all it takes to ensure your makeup stays put for hours on end — while feeling completely weightless on the skin.

There are so many formulas to consider, and not all are created equal. To help you find the perfect setting spray for your skin type, we enlisted the help of a handful of reviewers to research the most popular options and try them out firsthand in our lab. We spritzed our way through 28 setting sprays — keeping finish, formula and lasting power top of mind — to find the 11 best options. In the end, the Benefit Cosmetics Super Setter Pore-Minimizing Setting Spray caught our attention because of its pore-blurring finish and long-lasting hold, even after being splashed with water.

Keep reading to find the full list of best makeup setting sprays, according to InStyle testers.

Our Testing Process

To start, we spent about a week researching the top-rated setting sprays on the market. After honing in on online ratings, we spent 30 hours putting the 28 most popular setting sprays to the test. We had each of our testers try the setting sprays over makeup so they could analyze the performance of each one and write down their insights during the process. If any product claimed to be waterproof, the testers splashed water on their face, then dabbed with a towel to note makeup that ran or transferred. In doing so, our testers rated each setting spray from a scale of 1 to 5 for three key categories: comfort, performance, and water resistance. Once the data was collected, we were able to narrow down the original pool of products to the 11 best setting sprays.

What to Keep in Mind

Ingredients

When it comes to selecting the right formula, professional makeup artist Jonet Williamson suggests starting with the ingredient list. From there, you can tailor it to your skin's needs and your glam style. While formulas containing alcohol can prolong makeup, they can be irritating, especially if you use it everyday. Be aware that a little goes a long way and, if you're sensitive to alcohol-based products, look for an alcohol-free formula.

Skin Type

"There's a setting spray for every skin type," Williamson shares. "If you're someone with dry skin, you'll want to use a setting spray with hydrating ingredients like essential oils and hyaluronic acid for added moisture (like the Rare Beauty Always An Optimist Mist); but for oily skin, you may want to use a setting spray that will also help control shine (like the ONE/SIZE Waterproof Setting Spray)."

Staying Power

As far as setting sprays are concerned, staying power is key — you want one that will ensure your makeup stays in place for as long as possible. Finishing sprays can offer hold from anywhere between 12 to 24 hours. (Our Best Overall pick, the Benefit Super Setter Setting Spray, has a staying power of up to 16 hours.) Consider how long you intend to wear your makeup and your location — if it will be hot out, if you will be sweating etc. These factors will help determine how long-lasting your setting spray should be.

Your Questions, Answered

How do you use a setting spray?

Regardless of your makeup expertise, Williamson assures InStyle that setting sprays can fit seamlessly into your existing routine. "Ideally, you'll want this to be the final step in your makeup routine," she explains. "Sprays should be spritzed about six inches from your face so that it falls lightly onto the skin." For some, it might be beneficial to prime the skin with the same spray or finish up with a sweep of powder to ensure long-wearing results. "A trick that I like to do for my clients with oily skin is to spray a layer of setting spray before and after makeup," Williamson adds. "For makeup that holds up under masks, I'd suggest double setting, which means using a setting powder and spray."

Can you apply setting spray to other parts of the body?

It's also important to note that setting spray isn't exclusively for your face makeup; it can also be applied to the eyes and lips. In addition to locking your glam in place, Williamson recommends using a setting spray to take your eyeshadow to new heights. "Setting sprays can also come in handy when doing your eye makeup," she says. "It can turn a matte shadow into a cream liner and intensify the color payoff of shimmery shadows." To put this tip into action: dip your brush into the powder first, then give a spritz of setting spray before tapping onto your lids or swiping across your lash line — you'll notice the impact immediately.
