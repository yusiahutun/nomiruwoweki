Where is Ice Spice from?

The personality was born Isis Naija Gaston on 1 January 2000 (age 23 as of 2023) in The Bronx, New York City, United States. She grew up in a single-parent household in the Fordham Road neighbourhood. Spice describes her childhood as fun but chaotic.

Her parents worked all the time and had little time for her. So, Ice went to live with her cousins and grandparents. Then, she attended a school in the Bronx, where she completed her early childhood education.

When she was seven years old, she developed a liking for hip-hop. She listened to a cross-section of Latin artists like Aventura mixed with hip-hop artists like Jay-Z, Wu-Tang Clan, and Jadakiss.

Spice also listened to many top female rappers like Nicki Minaj and used their music styles to write her songs. Eventually, she became good at freestyle and poetry, continuing the trend to high school.

After that, she enrolled at Sacred Heart High School, a private Roman Catholic high school in Yonkers, New York. She continued with her talent for writing songs and even selected the name Ice Spice (Ice is short for her first name, Isis) as her stage name.

After graduating high school in 2018, she enrolled at the State University of New York at Purchase (SUNY Purchase). She studied Biology at SUNY Purchase and later did communications. She engaged in sports and became a defensive specialist on the school’s volleyball team.

Who are Ice Spice’s parents?

Her parents are Joseph Gaston (father) and Charina Almanzar (mother). Joseph and Charina met at a McDonald’s when Charina was still a teenager. She got pregnant with Ice Spice when she was 17. The couple separated two years later when Ice Spice was two years old.

Joseph Gaston is an African American with Nigerian roots, and Charina Almanzar is of Dominican descent. Ice Spice's father worked as an underground rapper and played an instrumental role in shaping Ice Spice’s career.
