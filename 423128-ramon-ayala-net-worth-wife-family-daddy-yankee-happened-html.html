Ramón Ayala biography: Who is Ramón Ayala?

The legendary Ramón Ayala, popularly known as "King of the Accordion," was born as Ramón Covarrubias Garza in Monterrey, Nuevo León in Mexico on December 8, 1945. His parents are Ramón Covarrubias and Natalia Garza.

Covarrubias was a local Mexican musician and played a pivotal role in Ramon’s formative years as a budding artist. Covarrubias's accordion gift to his five-year-old Ayala stoked his passion for music.

In 1959, Ramón’s parents moved to the northern state of Tamaulipas to work in the cotton fields. To support his struggling parents, Ayala worked as a shoeshine boy outside a local bar. Slowly, he became drawn to their ballads and the musicians who played at the joint.

While attending a performance, Ramon met Cornelius Reyna. Reyna was then a budding young guitarist who played for different bands. The friendship that culminated from their meeting was a turning point to then 15-year-old Ayala.

Apart from being an accredited artist, he is also a music composer and songwriter. His success in the Norteño genre translated into immense record sales realized throughout his long musical career.

The Ranchera style is popular in Texas and Mexico. Without insight into this Spanish-inspired style, it is hard to distinguish between the Norteño and Tejano genres. These Spanish tunes have attracted great listeners among Mexican American community and as far as Colombia. The ballads have an overpowering sense of harmony, hence more appealing even to non-Hispanics.

Formation of Los Relampagos Del Norte band and journey to the US

Cornelio and Ramon cemented their friendship. Later the duo established the famous Los Relampagos Del Norte (The Lightning from the North). They hit it off well and soon started recording accordion music of conjunto genre, which had roots in Texas, but at the time, was deemed independent of Norteño style.

Luck would later smile on their way. Paulino Bernal, the co-founder of Conjunto Bernal, gave the duo an offer: of a ticket to cross the border to the US to record their album. They accepted the offer. With financial assistance from Paulino, the two musicians applied for visas, enabling them smooth travel to the US.

In 1963, Los Relámpagos del Norte compiled their first album that featured the hit track, Ya No Llores (Don’t Cry Anymore). What followed were performance tours in several states including, California, Washington, and Florida. The success of that followed enticed the band to release eight more albums, which managed to top the charts.

More musical alliances and success

The astronomical rise of Ramon and his group came with its fair share of challenges. Cornelio Reyna left the group in 1971 to pursue a solo career in mariachi music. However, vocalist, Antonio Sauceda, joined the band to fill the position vacated by Cornelio. The changes necessitated the rebranding of the band as Ramón Ayala y sus Bravos Del Norte.

What happened Ramón Ayala?

Is Ramón Ayala still alive? Yes. Ramon recently celebrated more than 30 years in the music industry. He seemed to have faded from the music scene, with many of his fans reminiscing the glory years of Ramón Ayala y sus Bravos Del Norte. However, even at his advanced age of 70, he still performs at many concerts and shows, albeit with rare mention of upcoming albums.

Music runs deep in the family. True to this statement, he often makes appearances on stage with his daughter, Yesenia Ayala. Not left behind are Ramon Ayala's grandchildren, including Christopher Ayala, who played the accordion in 2019 alongside his grandfather to a crowd of 75,000 in Houston.

Ramón Ayala's family

Ayala has been married for more than 35 years and is a father to four children. His wife, Linda Ayala, is a member of the Hidalgo City Council since 2018.

Ramon Ayala Jr looks to be the only child who followed his dad's footsteps in music. Apart from being a member of Los Bravos del Norte, he released an album in 1987, followed by several popular singles. On the other hand, daughter, Yesenia Ayala, vied for a seat at Hidalgo School Board and won in the 2012 elections.

Is Ramón Ayala Daddy Yankee's father?

No. The "King of the Accordion" is not the rapper's father. Daddy Yankee, popular reggeton artist and father to Yamilet Ayala Gonzales, was born in Puerto Rico and son to Ramón Ayala and Rosa Rodriguez. The confusion stems from the similarity in names and his father's affiliation with salsa bands as a drummer.
