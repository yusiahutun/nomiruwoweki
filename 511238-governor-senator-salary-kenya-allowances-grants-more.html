Governor vs senator salary in Kenya: Allowances, grants, and more

 Posted on February 23, 2024  |  6 minutes  |  1139 words  |  Reinaldo Massengill

Before promulgating the Constitution of Kenya 2010, Kenyan politicians passed laws to boost their income. This greed, however, was slightly alleviated when the Salaries and Remuneration Commission was established in the 2010 Constitution. The SRC controls the wages of all public officers, including governors and senators. But have you ever taken a closer look at governor vs senator salary in Kenya?

Governors and senators in Kenya serve their respective counties. A governor manages resources allocated to the county by the national government. He is the chief executive of the county government and is responsible for ensuring that county's laws and regulations are followed.

A senator represents the interests of the counties and their governments. Senators make laws approving bills concerning counties and determine the allocation of national revenue among counties. Since they have different roles, the governor vs senator salary in Kenya differs.

The SRC determines the salaries, grants, and allowances paid to governors and senators. When you look at the governor vs senator salary in Kenya, governors earn more than senators.

The salaries are not constant because the law requires the Salaries and Remuneration Commission to reviewthe salary of every public official after two fiscal years based on the status of the economy.

Governor salary in Kenya

A governor elected by Kenyans and sworn into office is entitled to a monthly consolidated remuneration package of Ksh 924,000. Governors also receive the following allowances and benefits from the Kenyan government:

1. Official transport

Governors receive official cars from the Kenyan government. The car engine must have a capacity of 3000cc.

2. Commuter allowance

The commuter allowance does not apply to governors because they already have an official vehicle.

3. Medical benefits

Medical benefits are part of the governor salary in Kenya. The benefits cover the governor, their spouse, and up to four children under twenty-five years, fully dependent on the governor.

The governor receives an inpatient cover of Ksh 10 million, outpatient cover of Ksh 300,000, maternity cover of Ksh 150,000, dental cover of Ksh 75,000 and optical cover of Ksh 75,000.

4. Retirement benefit

A governor in Kenya is eligible to service gratuity at the rate of 31% of annual pensionable emoluments.

5. Group life insurance

Governors receive group life insurance cover and group personal accident cover equivalent to three times their annual pensionable emoluments.

6. Airtime

Governors receive airtime worth Ksh 20,000 monthly.

7. Official residence

Governors receive an official residence constructed or purchased using public funds.

8. Security

Governors receive security as advised by the Inspector General of Police.

9. Annual leave allowance

Governors are not eligible for annual leave allowance.

10. Committee sitting allowance

Governors are not eligible for committee sitting allowance.

11. Mileage claim

Governors are not eligible for a mileage claim.

12. Car loan and mortgage benefit

A governor in Kenya is eligible for a car loan of up to Ksh 10 million and a mortgage benefit of up to Ksh 40 million.

13. Special responsibility allowance

Governors are not eligible for a special responsibility allowance.

Senators salary in Kenya

A senator sworn into office is entitled to a monthly salary of KES 710,000. If the senator serves as Leader of the Minority Party or Majority Party in the Senate, they receive KES 768,000. Senators receive the following allowances and benefits:

1. Official transport

Senators receive official cars from the Kenyan government. However, the car engine must have a capacity of 3000cc.

2. Commuter allowance

The senator salary in Kenya does not include commuter allowance because they have an official government vehicle.

3. Medical benefits

Medical benefits cover the senator, their spouse, and up to four children under twenty-five years, fully dependent on the senator. The senator receives an inpatient cover of KES 10 million, outpatient cover of KES 300,000, maternity cover of KES 150,000, dental cover of KES 100,000, and optical cover of KES 100,000.

4. Retirement benefit

A senator in Kenya is eligible for retirement benefits based on the law granting pensions to persons who have been members of the National Assembly and their dependents.

5. Group life insurance and group personal accident

Senators receive group life insurance cover and group personal accident cover, whose value is equivalent to three times their annual pension.

6. Airtime

Senators receive airtime worth KES 15,000 monthly.

7. Official residence

Senators do not receive an official residence.

8. Security

Senators receive security as advised by the Inspector General of Police.

9. Annual leave allowance

Senators are not eligible for annual leave allowance because they are elected officials.

10. Committee sitting allowance

The committee sitting allowance varies depending on their position on the committee. A senator who is the chairperson of a committee receives KES 15,000 per sitting. The maximum they can receive is KES 240,000 monthly.

A senator serving as the vice-chairperson of a committee receives an allowance of KES 12,000 per sitting; the maximum is 192,000 monthly. A senator who is a committee member receives an allowance of KES 7,500. However, the maximum is KES 120,000 monthly.

11. Mileage claim

Senators are eligible for a mileage claim of one return journey weekly from Nairobi to their respective county offices. However, the rate of the claim is KES 116.63 per kilometre. Also, their car engine must have a capacity of not more than 3000cc.

12. Car loan and mortgage benefit

A senator in Kenya is eligible for a car loan of up to KES 8 million and a mortgage benefit of up to KES 35 million.

13. Special responsibility allowance

Senators are eligible for a special responsibility allowance of up to KES 150,000. However, they receive the money if they perform their added responsibilities beyond the duties stipulated in their terms of service.

Governor vs senator salary in Kenya - Wrapping up

Governors and senators in Kenya have different roles allocated to them by the constitution. Because of their different duties, governor vs senator salary in Kenya vary. Per the SRC, a Kenyan governor gets a monthly consolidated remuneration package of KES 924,000, while a senator receives KES 710,000.

Tuko.co.ke published an informative article about MPs' salaries in Kenya, monthly allowances, and grants. The Salaries and Remuneration Commission (SRC) have been mandated to set the MP salaries in Kenya.

An MP's monthly base wage in Kenya is currently Ksh.710,000. The salary does not include benefits such as a car grant, mileage allowance, and sitting allowance that stands at Ksh.5,000 for every day the MP attends parliamentary sessions.
