60 Best Easter Songs for a Day of Worship 2024

 Posted on September 28, 2024  |  6 minutes  |  1127 words  |  Larita Shotwell

1

Popular Easter Hymn

"Nothing But the Blood of Jesus" by Carrie Underwood

1

Popular Easter Hymn

"Nothing But the Blood of Jesus" by Carrie Underwood

The queen of country will get everyone singing along to this Christian hymn. Written by Robert Lowry in 1876, the song appears on Underwood's 2021 album My Savior. "What can wash away my sin? Nothing but the blood of Jesus," go the lyrics.

2

Gospel Favorite

"Victory" by Yolanda Adams

2

Gospel Favorite

"Victory" by Yolanda Adams

Get ready to get up on your feet and dance in praise (you really won't be able to stop yourself) to this track from Adams's 2005 album Day by Day. The Easter message is especially prominent in the chorus: "For me he died but he rose on the third day / That's why I have true victory every day."

3

Popular Easter Hymn

"In the Garden" by Alan Jackson

3

Popular Easter Hymn

"In the Garden" by Alan Jackson

This hymn is written in the voice of Mary Magdalene, as if she's walking and talking with the risen savior. Penned by Austin Miles in 1912, "In the Garden" has become a traditional Easter song that remains popular in church services. Here, country singer Alan Jackson captured it beautifully on his album Precious Memories, released in 2016.

"King Of Kings" by Brooke Ligertwood and Hillsong Worship

Music for Easter Scripture Reading

8

Mighty To Save (Live)

8

Mighty To Save (Live)

"Mighty to Save” is a song that came not from one situation or circumstance in our lives, but as the sum of many. Our God who is all powerful, chooses not to turn his power against us, but towards us, drawing us to himself, writing us into a new story—his salvation story. We have seen it in our own lives." - Ben Fielding

9

Choral Music for Easter Sunday

"Jesus Christ Is Risen Today" by The Jubilate Singers

9

Choral Music for Easter Sunday

"Jesus Christ Is Risen Today" by The Jubilate Singers

Originally titled "Hymn for Easter Day," this song was written by Charles Wesley in 1739. The Jubilate Singers, a Toronto-based chamber choir, offer a beautiful, classic rendition. The intro lyrics say it all: "Jesus Christ is risen today, Allelujah! Our triumphant holy day, Allelujah!"

11

Choral Music for Easter Sunday

"Up From the Grave He Arose" by the Scottish Festival Singers

This hymn sums up what the Easter celebration is all about: "He arose! He arose! Hallelujah! Christ arose!" This choral version was recorded at the Old St. Paul's Church in Edinburgh for the album The Hymn Makers.

Contemporary Christian Easter Song

"Redeemer" by Nicole C. Mullen
