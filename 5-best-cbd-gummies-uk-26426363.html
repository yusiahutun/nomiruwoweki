What is CBD?

CBD (cannabidiol) is extracted from the hemp plant in the cannabis family, but rest assured - that doesn’t mean you’ll get high from gummies containing it. The cannabinoid responsible for the psychoactive effects of cannabis is THC, which you’ll find in marijuana plants. CBD is extracted from hemp, which has only trace amounts of THC.

Further still, to be sold legally in the UK, hemp products and CBD edibles have to contain less than 0.02% THC. For this reason, manufacturers filter any notable remaining amount of THC from CBD extracts during the extraction process.

CBD need-to-knows

How much should you take?

It’s best to check with your medical professional before starting out, but the FSA recommends no more than 70mg a day. Some users like to start with a lower dose, and then work their way up depending on how the body responds.

Which type of CBD should you look for?

All CBD gummies aren’t created equal, and the specific type you go for will depend on your personal needs.

At the beginning of the article, we mentioned that CBD isn’t the only cannabinoid in the hemp plant - there are other minor cannabinoids (like CBG and CBN) that, when taken at the same time as CBD, boost each other’s effectiveness. This is called the ‘entourage effect’.

  • Full spectrum CBD is the rawest form of CBD extract. That means it comes with other cannabinoids present in the hemp plant. This can lead to a more botanical taste, and higher levels of THC.
  • Broad spectrum CBD is similar to full spectrum: it’s packed with other cannabinoids. However, the manufacturer has filtered out the THC. This means it comes in under the legal UK limit of less than 0.02% THC, but still delivers the entourage effect.
  • CBD isolate has gone through a crystallisation process to strip out all other compounds, meaning your dose will contain just pure CBD. This can be a good option if you don’t want to ingest even trace amounts of THC, but it means you won’t experience the entourage effect.
